Top 3 Places in Sabah

Sipadan Island

1. Sipadan Island

Sipadan Island is among the world's top dive sites. Dive into its crystal-clear waters to discover exotic marine life, including sea turtles and coral reefs. Explore renowned diving spots such as Barracuda Point, Turtle Cave, and Coral Gardens, where sharks, green turtles, schools of jackfish, and barracudas await. Alternatively, soak in the stunning beauty of the island and its surrounding seas.

Mount Kinabalu

2. Mount Kinabalu

Mount Kinabalu, standing at 4,095 meters, is the tallest peak in Southeast Asia and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Renowned for its rich biodiversity, it features unique flora and fauna spread across distinct ecological zones. Adventurers can hike its well-maintained trails to enjoy breathtaking views and witness the magical sunrise at Lowâ€™s Peak.

Kinabalu National Park

3. Kinabalu National Park

Not a fan of hiking? Visit Kinabalu Park to immerse yourself in flora and fauna, with wildlife ranging from mammals and birds to fishes, amphibians, reptiles, insects, and other invertebrates. Among the 29 bird species unique to Borneo, at least 17 are confined to the mountainous region, notably Kinabalu. Prefer a more relaxing experience? Head to Poring Hot Spring, located 40 km from the park's headquarters, to unwind in natural sulphur baths.
