Kedah: Land of Paddy Fields and Tradition

Kedah, located in the north of Peninsular Malaysia, is renowned for its golden paddy fields with centuries-old traditions. Often called the “Rice Bowl of Malaysia,” it plays a vital role in the nation’s agriculture. Kedah is also home to historical treasures, traditional crafts, and the iconic Langkawi islands, blending nature, history, and tradition in one charming state.

Langkawi Island

Pulau Langkawi is a gem of Kedah, famous for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, and lush landscapes. With 99 islands to explore, it offers a perfect mix of nature, adventure, and relaxation. From the stunning Sky Bridge to duty-free shopping, Langkawi has something for everyone.

    • Zahir Mosque

    Zahir Mosque, located in Alor Setar, Kedah, is one of Malaysia’s oldest and most beautiful mosques. Built in 1912, its stunning Moorish-inspired architecture, black domes, and elegant arches make it a symbol of Islamic heritage and a landmark of Kedah.

    • Alor Setar Tower

    Alor Setar Tower, or Menara Alor Setar, is a prominent landmark in Kedah's capital city. Standing at 165.5 meters, it serves as both a telecommunications tower and a tourist attraction. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Alor Setar and even the Langkawi islands from its observation deck.
