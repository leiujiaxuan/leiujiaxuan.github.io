Top 3 Places in Sarawak

Bako National Park

1. Bako National Park

Bako National Park is a must-visit destination, known for its incredible biodiversity and stunning landscapes. It's a great place to explore rainforests, mangroves, and secluded beaches. Spot the silvered leaf monkeys, monitor lizards and squirrels and flying lemurs. You can see bearded pigs as you walk Bakoâ€™s trails.

Gunung Mulu National Park

2. Gunung Mulu National Park

Gunung Mulu National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning caves and karst formations. It features one of the world's largest limestone cave systems, including record-breaking attractions like Deer Cave, Sarawak Chamber, and Clearwater Cave. The park also boasts lush rainforests, diverse wildlife, and challenging treks, such as the Pinnacles hike. It's a paradise for adventurers and nature enthusiasts alike!

Sarawak Cultural Village

3. Sarawak Cultural Village

Sarawak Cultural Village is an award-winning 'living museum' that showcases the rich heritage of Sarawak's diverse ethnic groups. It features authentic replicas of traditional longhouses and buildings representing communities like the Iban, Bidayuh, Melanau, and more. You can delve into local cultures and engage in hands-on activities like beadwork making, paddy pounding, and even enjoy captivating cultural performances.
