Top 3 Places in Melaka

Christ Church Melaka

1. Dutch Square (Red Square)

Dutch Square, also called Red Square, is a famous spot in Melaka, known for its bright red buildings. It's a great place to visit historical landmarks like Christ Church and Stadthuys or check out the pretty Queen Victoria Fountain. You'll also find colorful trishaws, souvenir shops, and plenty of spots to snap photos. Take a walk around to soak in the unique mix of Dutch and British history in one charming area.

Saint Paul's Church

2. St. Paul's Hill & Church

St. Paul's Hill, also known as Bukit St. Paul, it was originally called Malacca Hill during the Sultanate period and later renamed Mary's Hill by the Portuguese. It was subsequently renamed St. Paul's Hill by the Dutch in honor of the church at its summit. At the summit, you'll find the ruins of St. Paul's Church, one of the oldest European church structure in Southeast Asia, built in 1521. Outside the church, there is a big white statue of St. Francis Xavier, a Catholic missionary from Spain who traveled across Asia to spread Christianity. A Famosa

3. A Famosa

A Famosa, meaning "The Famous" in Portuguese, is one of Melaka's most iconic historical landmarks. Built in 1511 by the Portuguese, it was originally a massive fortress designed to protect their settlement and control trade routes through the Straits of Malacca. Today, only the Porta de Santiago gate remains, offering a glimpse into its grand past. Around A Famosa, you might come across street performers, such as someone posing as a golden statue.
